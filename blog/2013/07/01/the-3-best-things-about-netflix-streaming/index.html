The 3 Best Things About Netflix Streaming

Jul 1st, 2013

Netflix streaming gets a bad rap sometimes. Here are three reasons for why having a streaming plan is not so bad. In fact, it’s pretty good.

  1. Season/Series Catch-Up
  2. Foreign Movies & Television
  3. Original Programming

Reason #1: Season / Series Catch-Up

I have a confession to make. I did not watch Breaking Bad when it premiered. As someone born and raised in DC and seeing the effects of crime and drugs in the community, I couldn’t see how watching a high school chemistry teacher with terminal cancer from Albuquerque, morph into a kingpin meth dealer would be appealing to me. But everyone was talking about it, so I thought I’d give it a try. And a one episode experiment turned into a binge.

I also didn’t watch The Walking Dead. I like zombies, but not that much. I started watching at the suggestion of friend who said it wasn’t just about zombies. And she was right. It’s not really about zombies. If there is ever a zombie apocalypse, the zombies are the least of your worries. Watch out for the charismatic man who wears an eyepatch and whistles creepily.

Those are just two examples. Other great series to watch: American Horror Story, 30 Rock, It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia and The West Wing.

Reason #2: Foreign Movies & Televison

Let’s face it, American movies are entertaining but they lack originality. You know what’s going to happen because it’s the same story told over and over again. I find foreign films are much more original and have some of the best acting you’ll ever see from people you’ve never heard of. It’s not surprising Hollywood remakes foreign films and not just the horror flicks (e.g. The Ring, The Grudge).

If you liked the The Girl With the Dragon Tattoo (US) and haven’t seen the original trilogy, you owe it to yourself to watch it. Spike Lee is remaking the film Oldboy starring Josh Brolin (I’m not sure how I feel about this). Watch the Korean version first.

If you’re into CSI, NCIS and Criminal Minds, you’ll love British crime/drama. Some of my favorites: Sherlock, Luther, Wallander, and Wallander (original Swedish version).

Reason #3: Original Progamming

This is the least compelling reason, but I think it will get better. House of Cards with Kevin Spacey (based on the British television show) was an instant hit when it debuted in February becoming Netflix’s most streamed show. But not only was the show good, Netflix did something unconventional by releasing the entire season at once. Amazon’s Instant Video service has since followed the same model.

If you’re a Twilight fan (I’m not), you may like Hemlock Grove (I didn’t). Other Netflix produced shows include: Orange is the New Black, Lilyhammer and Arrested Development (not original but revived).

So there you have it. There are more than enough great shows to watch that will keep you paying the $8/month it costs for a subscription.
